## My Personal Avoid List

**Clickbait hooks**
- Hooks that tease without delivering
- "You won't believe…"
- "This will change everything…"
- "Here's what nobody tells you…"
- Any opener that withholds the point to manufacture curiosity

**Hyperbolic adjectives**
- revolutionary
- game-changing
- groundbreaking
- world-class
- cutting-edge
- next-level
- mind-blowing
- unprecedented
- extraordinary (when used as filler praise)

**Press release language**
- "thrilled to announce"
- "excited to share"
- "proud to introduce"
- "we are pleased to inform"
- "officially launching"
- "it is with great pleasure"

**Technical jargon without explanation**
- Any acronym dropped without context
- Industry terms used to signal credibility rather than add clarity
- Buzzword stacks: "AI-powered end-to-end integrated solution"

**Interchangeable content — rewrite if any of these apply**
- The post could be published by a SaaS founder, a coach, and a consultant without changing a word
- No specific number, name, rupee amount, or personal observation grounds the claim
- The lesson could appear in any industry newsletter unchanged

## Patterns Detected in My Posts to Watch

**Opener drift**
- Restating the hook in the second sentence instead of advancing it — kills momentum
- Adding "I realised this by accident" too often — works once per month, not once per post
- Starting with "Here's the thing:" — sounds like every other LinkedIn voice

**List structure fatigue**
- Numbered lists where every point is the same weight — no escalation, no tonal shift at the end
- Padding lists past 8 points when 5 would land harder
- Using list format when a short paragraph would hit faster

**Closing line drift**
- Ending with "Would love to hear your thoughts" — directly against the voice
- Closing with a summary instead of a lesson or a challenge
- Two-sentence closings — the second sentence always weakens the first, cut it

**Rhythm collapse**
- Writing three long sentences in a row — loses the staccato punch
- Forgetting to place the short sentence after the setup pair
- Using commas to extend a sentence that should have been split

**Contrast pair overuse**
- Leaning on myth vs. truth, smart vs. not-so-smart in every post
- Using the → arrow more than four times in a single post
- Binary choice closings ("If X → do this. If Y → do that.") appearing in back-to-back posts

**Generic personal proof**
- "I've been building for X years and here's what I learned" — strips the specific story
- Name-dropping a client or brand without making it do argumentative work
- Vulnerability hooks that don't connect to the actual lesson of the post

**Category-level abstractions without the ground-level detail first**
- Opening at the market or societal level before earning it with a personal observation
- Making a system-level claim with no rupee amount, no named company, no real number anchoring it

## Core AI-Tell Defaults (Always Ban These)

**Words**
- delve
- leverage
- synergy
- utilize
- innovative
- game-changing
- revolutionary
- pivotal
- seamless
- transformative
- nuanced
- impactful
- groundbreaking
- embark
- foster
- harness
- unlock
- elevate
- showcase
- resonate

**Phrases**
- "thrilled to announce"
- "excited to share"
- "I'm honored"
- "let that sink in"
- "it's important to note"
- "in today's fast-paced world"
- "the funny part?" followed by something not funny

**Structures**
- Em dashes used as sentence asides — rewrite as two sentences or use a colon
- Emoji bullet points
- Hashtag spam (hashtags mid-body copy or more than three at the end)
- Fake vulnerability hooks (manufactured confession that exists only to lower defenses before a pitch)
