Macy's, a $21.7B retailer, just shipped an AI shopping assistant which helps users spend 4.75x more. The tool is called "Ask Macy's," and it is powered by Google Gemini. They tested it with half their website visitors over several weeks before going live and here's how they've designed it to increase AOV: 1. You don't get a single product, you get a complete outfit. If a user types "I need something for a wedding," it pulls the dress, shoes, and accessories together in one response. Cross-selling by design. 2. Virtual try-on lets you see how items look on you before buying, removing the biggest reason people drop fashion carts: not knowing if something actually works on them. 3. It asks follow-ups on budget, occasion, color, and size before showing anything. Instead of flooding you with options, it narrows intent first. Closer to how a store associate works than a search bar. IMO the 4.75x is probably inflated, but the logic behind it is solid: - AI in commerce doesn't just capture demand, it creates it. A shopper who talks through what to wear to a wedding buys more than someone scrolling a category page. - The 4.75x is early and likely skewed by high-intent users. But even at 2x, each AI-assisted visit generates double the revenue per session. Macy's revenue dropped 2.4% last year, for them this could be a real lever.
