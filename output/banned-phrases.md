## My Personal Avoid List

**Clickbait hooks**
- Any opener engineered to withhold information to force a click
- "You won't believe..."
- "This changes everything..."
- "Nobody is talking about this"
- "I need to share this"

**Hyperbolic adjectives**
- Revolutionary
- Game-changing
- Groundbreaking
- Unprecedented
- Extraordinary
- Remarkable (when used as a substitute for showing why something matters)

**Press release language**
- "Thrilled to announce"
- "Excited to share"
- "We are pleased to"
- "Proud to announce"
- "I am delighted to"

**Technical jargon without explanation**
- Any acronym used without at least one contextual anchor on first use
- Any industry term dropped in to signal expertise rather than to clarify

**Interchangeable filler — the swap test**
- If a post could be published by someone in a different industry without changing a word, rewrite it
- Generic lesson statements: "This is why X matters," "The lesson here is clear"
- Industry-agnostic motivational closes: "Keep pushing," "Stay curious," "Do the work"

## Patterns Detected in My Posts to Watch

**Opener drift**
- Resist letting "Lead with a concrete number" calcify into a formula — vary how the number is framed, not just what it is
- "Here's what [X] found" is a useful transitional phrase but flags as a tic if it appears in every post; limit to one per post
- "Here's why that makes sense" carries the same risk — it can become throat-clearing before the actual point

**Repetitive structural signals**
- "That means..." used more than once per post starts to feel mechanical
- "IMO" as a section opener is a structural marker, not a casual hedge — do not scatter it mid-paragraph to soften claims
- Numbered lists used by default rather than by fit — not every post needs a numbered list; use it only when sequence or ranking genuinely exists

**Phrases any LinkedIn user could post unchanged**
- "Here's what caught my attention" — only earns its place if what follows is genuinely specific
- "It's a smart move" — shows nothing; replace with the mechanism that makes it smart
- "Worth paying attention to" — vague; say what specifically to watch for and why
- "This is big" — does no analytical work; the numbers should do that job instead
- "Things are moving fast" — too general to mean anything in context

**Scale anchoring gone wrong**
- Attaching a number to a company name is a habit — do not attach numbers that are old, unverified, or irrelevant to the point being made
- Scale markers used decoratively rather than to establish credibility should be cut

**Fragment overuse**
- Deliberate fragments work when they reset pace; three or more in a row tips into affectation
- "That's it. You're caught up." is a useful closer pattern but becomes a cliché if it appears in post after post

**Interpretation section drift**
- The "IMO" section exists to separate data from opinion — do not let it drift into motivational territory or soft calls-to-action disguised as analysis

## Core AI-Tell Defaults (Always Ban These)

**Words**
- Delve
- Leverage
- Synergy
- Utilize
- Innovative
- Game-changing
- Revolutionary
- Pivotal
- Seamless
- Transformative
- Nuanced
- Impactful
- Groundbreaking
- Embark
- Foster
- Harness
- Unlock
- Elevate
- Showcase
- Resonate

**Phrases**
- "Thrilled to announce"
- "Excited to share"
- "I'm honored"
- "Let that sink in"
- "It's important to note"
- "In today's fast-paced world"
- "The funny part?" followed by something not funny

**Structures**
- Em dashes — rewrite the sentence or use a colon instead
- Emoji bullet points
- Hashtag spam
- Fake vulnerability hooks
