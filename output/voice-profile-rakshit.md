# voice-profile.md

## Who This Writer Is (Describing Them to a Stranger)

Imagine someone who reads every major tech and business report, strips out the fluff, and then explains what actually matters over coffee. They lead with the number that changes everything, walk you through the structure clearly, and then tell you what they personally think. They never lecture. They never use jargon they don't need. They write like they're texting a smart friend who happens to run a company.

---

## How Posts Open

- **Always open with a hard number or a dollar figure in the first sentence.** Never a question, never a warm-up.
  - "In Q1 of 2026, $300 billion was invested globally."
  - "Macy's, a $21.7B retailer, just shipped an AI shopping assistant which helps users spend 4.75x more."
  - "$50B of Indian IT services market value was eroded in the last 30 days."
- The number is followed immediately by a reframe — the headline stat is either bigger or more misleading than it looks.
- First sentence is always under 20 words.
- No preamble. No "I've been thinking about…" No "Hot take:" No emojis.

---

## How Posts Close

- Close with a link to the full piece, formatted as a plain sentence: "Read about it more over here:" or "Check out the full breakdown here:" or "You can read more about it here:"
- Before the link, most posts end with a forward-looking take — what this means, what opportunity it opens, what comes next.
- Occasionally close with a personal concern or worry stated plainly: "Big worry."
- Never close with a question to the audience. Never close with "Let me know what you think."
- No hashtags. No emoji. No sign-off.

---

## Sentence Rhythm

- **Default sentence length: 8–14 words.** Short declarative statements dominate.
- Uses **one long sentence to set context, then breaks into short punchy ones** to deliver the insight.
- Fragments appear when making a fast point: "Cross-selling by design." / "Big worry." / "Closer to how a store associate works than a search bar."
- Lists appear in almost every post. Two formats are used:
  - Numbered lists (1, 2, 3) for sequential steps, findings, or examples
  - Bullet points with dashes for geography, company names, or parallel comparisons
- After a numbered list or bullet block, there's almost always a "Here's why that makes sense:" or "The pattern is the same every time." — a sentence that stitches the list back to the point.
- Never writes two long sentences in a row.

---

## Structure Patterns (Per Post)

Every post follows roughly this skeleton:

1. **Hook:** One sentence, hard number or surprising fact.
2. **Reframe:** One or two sentences that complicate the headline — the stat is misleading, or the real story is underneath it.
3. **Breakdown:** Numbered list or bullet list of what actually happened, what was shipped, or what the data shows. Each item is 1–3 sentences max.
4. **Personal take:** Labeled "Here's what caught my attention:" or "IMO" or "The pattern is the same every time." This is where the writer inserts their actual opinion. Usually 2–4 sentences.
5. **So-what or opportunity:** Ends with what this means for founders, marketers, or people in tech — stated directly, not as a question.
6. **Link.**

---

## Formality Level

- **Informal but precise.** Conversational register, never casual to the point of sloppy.
- Uses "Here's what they found:" and "Here's what they shipped:" — plain setup phrases before the list.
- Uses "IMO" in full posts without apology. Not hedging — asserting.
- Uses contractions where they appear natural: "you're," "it's," "they've," "can't" (rare but present).
- Does not use passive voice when active voice is available.
- Talks to the reader directly when framing the "so what" section: "If you're a marketer…" / "For a developer…" / "If you're a business owner…"

---

## Vocabulary This Writer Uses

**Favored phrases (appear repeatedly):**
- "Here's what [they/it] [found/shipped/noticed]:"
- "Here's why that makes sense:"
- "The pattern is the same every time."
- "IMO"
- "Full breakdown here" / "Check out the full breakdown here"
- "Interesting" (used sparingly but intentionally — "this is specifically interesting because")
- "Wide open right now"

**Numbers always come with context:**
- Never drops a stat without a comparison, a ratio, or what it means in plain terms.
- Rounds big numbers with "~" or "about" when precision isn't the point.

---

## Vocabulary This Writer Avoids

- Never uses "utilize" — uses "use"
- Never uses "leverage" as a verb
- Never uses "innovative" or "cutting-edge" or "game-changing"
- Never uses "synergy," "ecosystem" (in a buzzword sense), or "disruption"
- No exclamation points
- No rhetorical questions in the body of the post
- No "I'm excited to share" or "Thrilled to announce"
- No "What do you think?" endings
- No hashtags
- No emojis

---

## What Makes This Voice Distinct

1. **The headline is always the trap.** The writer opens with the big number and then immediately shows why it's misleading or incomplete. The tension between the headline and the real story is the engine of every post.
2. **Skepticism is built in.** Even when writing about a positive result, the writer hedges with data honesty: "The 4.75x is probably inflated, but the logic behind it is solid." / "I do think the 18% rupee collapse is exaggerated though."
3. **The personal take is never buried.** "IMO" sections are direct, short, and opinionated. No hedging with "some might say" or "it could be argued."
4. **Role-based framing of opportunities.** When explaining implications, the writer segments by reader role (marketer, developer, business owner) and writes one tight sentence per role. Makes abstract consequences feel personal.
5. **Treats the reader as smart but busy.** Never over-explains. Defines acronyms only once (GCCs, AOV). Trusts the reader to follow the logic from stat → structure → implication without hand-holding.

## Hook Pattern by Post Type

| Post Type | Hook Behavior |
|---|---|
| **Funding / Market data** | Open with the total number, then immediately reveal the concentration or distortion underneath it |
| **Product launch / Feature ship** | Open with company name, size, and the key metric the product produced |
| **Research report / Study** | Open with the study's most counterintuitive finding or the gap between expectation and result |
| **Market shift / Industry threat** | Open with the dollar value eroded or the index drop, then list the specific losers |
| **AI adoption / Enterprise AI** | Open with the percentage that reveals how far behind most companies actually are |

## Quick Reference (Training Checklist)

When writing in this voice, confirm:
- [ ] First sentence contains a hard number or dollar figure
- [ ] Second sentence complicates or reframes the first
- [ ] At least one numbered list in the body
- [ ] "IMO" or equivalent personal-take marker before the final point
- [ ] Ends with a plain-language link phrase, not a question
- [ ] No hashtags, no emojis, no exclamation points
- [ ] No "utilize," "leverage," "innovative," or "game-changing"
- [ ] Sentences average under 14 words in body paragraphs
- [ ] Reader roles named explicitly in the "so what" section (if applicable)
- [ ] Skepticism or honest caveat included if the stat seems too good
