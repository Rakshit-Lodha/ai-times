POST: The Indian Perfume Industry Has Sold Us a Lie

The Indian Perfume Industry Has Sold Us a Lie. I realised this by accident. I have a cheap perfume called Spruce Shave Club – Blade. Bought it on CRED for under ₹500. I use it casually at home. Recently I also got Dior Sauvage (EDT). One day I sprayed Blade… and suddenly it felt familiar. The smell was almost identical. It hit me. Blade is basically cloning Sauvage. But here's the interesting part. Blade claims to be Eau De Parfum (EDP). Sauvage is just an Eau De Toilette (EDT). Now according to perfume marketing: EDP = stronger EDP = longer lasting EDT = weaker Right? Reality check. • Blade (EDP) lasted 3 hours • Sauvage (EDT) lasted 10+ hours Same smell family. Opposite performance. That's when I realised something. The Indian perfume industry has successfully vilified EDT. They convinced everyone that: EDT = weak EDP = premium So every brand now launches EDP. But concentration labels mean nothing without quality ingredients and formulation. A well-made designer EDT can destroy a poorly made EDP. Perfume isn't about the label. It's about raw materials, balance, and chemistry. Sometimes a ₹10,000 EDT beats a ₹500 EDP. And sometimes the ₹500 one just copies the smell… without the performance. Marketing sells the myth. Formulation decides the truth.
