## My Personal Avoid List

**Clickbait hooks**
- Any opener that withholds the point to manufacture suspense
- "You won't believe…"
- "This changes everything."
- "Nobody is talking about this."
- "Here's the truth they don't want you to know."

**Hyperbolic adjectives**
- revolutionary
- game-changing
- groundbreaking
- world-class
- unprecedented
- explosive (used to describe growth)
- jaw-dropping
- mind-blowing

**Press release language**
- "thrilled to announce"
- "excited to share"
- "proud to introduce"
- "we are pleased to announce"
- "officially launching"
- "delighted to reveal"

**Technical jargon without explanation**
- Any acronym used more than once without being defined on first use
- Industry shorthand dropped without a plain-English follow-up
- Buzzwords used as substitutes for a real claim (e.g., "AI-powered" without specifying what it actually does)

**Interchangeable statements — if anyone in any industry could post it unchanged, rewrite it**
- Generic observations about "the pace of change"
- Statements that could apply to any company, any market, any year
- Opinions with no number, name, or specific example attached
- Conclusions that could come from a press release rather than independent analysis

## Patterns Detected in My Posts to Watch

**Opener drift**
- Opening with a number but not immediately complicating or reframing it — the tension is the point; a stat without a reframe is just a stat
- Starting a second post the same week with the same dollar-figure format without a structural variation
- Letting "Here's what they found:" become a verbal tic that appears in every post regardless of whether a list follows

**Structural repetition**
- Overusing the exact sequence: hook → numbered list → "Here's why that makes sense:" → IMO → link — vary the skeleton occasionally
- Numbered lists that run longer than 5 items and stop feeling like a tight breakdown and start feeling like a data dump
- Every post ending with a "so what" framed by reader role — useful but can become formulaic if every closing beat hits marketer / developer / business owner in the same order

**Phrase fatigue — favored phrases that can go stale**
- "The pattern is the same every time." — powerful when earned, hollow when used as a default close
- "Wide open right now." — only use when the evidence in the post actually supports it
- "IMO" — should mark a genuine, specific opinion; avoid using it to introduce a vague or hedged take
- "Here's what caught my attention:" — can slip into sounding like a hook-writing template rather than an authentic reaction

**Skepticism that becomes performative**
- Adding a hedge ("the number is probably inflated") without actually explaining why — skepticism needs a reason
- Using honest caveats as a stylistic move rather than a genuine data check

**Interchangeable conclusions**
- "This is a space worth watching." — says nothing specific
- "The opportunity here is clear." — prove it or cut it
- Any final sentence that could be lifted and placed at the end of a different post without changing its meaning

**Tone drift toward lecture**
- Explaining what a finding "means for the industry" in abstract terms rather than naming a specific role or decision
- Writing "founders should…" or "marketers need to…" without grounding it in something from the post itself

## Core AI-Tell Defaults (Always Ban These)

**Words**
- delve
- leverage
- synergy
- utilize
- innovative
- game-changing
- revolutionary
- pivotal
- seamless
- transformative
- nuanced
- impactful
- groundbreaking
- embark
- foster
- harness
- unlock
- elevate
- showcase
- resonate

**Phrases**
- "thrilled to announce"
- "excited to share"
- "I'm honored"
- "let that sink in"
- "it's important to note"
- "in today's fast-paced world"
- "the funny part?" followed by something not funny

**Structures**
- Em dashes — rewrite the sentence or use a colon instead
- Emoji bullet points
- Hashtag spam
- Fake vulnerability hooks
