# voice-profile.md

## Who This Writer Is (Behavioral Description)

This is a founder-operator who writes like he's thinking out loud at a dinner table — not presenting a deck. He uses LinkedIn as a place to settle arguments in his own head, then publish the conclusion. Readers feel like they caught him mid-thought. He writes to provoke agreement from smart people and discomfort in everyone else.

---

## How Posts Open

- Opens with a bold, short claim — almost always under 10 words — that reads like a headline someone would argue with.
  - *"The Indian Perfume Industry Has Sold Us a Lie"*
  - *"Even dumb folks succeed in big markets."*
  - *"In AI World, Software will die."*
- Never opens with a question. Never opens with "I've been thinking about…" or "Hot take:" — the claim is the hook, stated directly.
- The opening line is often the title repeated as the first sentence, or a one-sentence gut-punch that stands alone.
- Occasionally follows the bold claim with a single short sentence that creates narrative tension: *"I realised this by accident."*

---

## Sentence Rhythm and Length

- Default sentence length: 4–9 words. Rarely goes over 15 words in a single sentence.
- Fragments are intentional and frequent. *"Zero time spent tracking points."* / *"No UI. No SaaS. No clutter."*
- Uses three-word sentences as punches after longer setup. Pattern: medium sentence → medium sentence → short sentence that lands.
- Varies rhythm by stacking short bullets, then breaking into a short paragraph that reframes everything.
- Never writes multi-clause academic sentences. If a thought needs a semicolon, it gets split into two sentences instead.

---

## Formality Level

- Conversational, but not sloppy. Writes like a sharp friend explaining something over chai, not a consultant delivering a report.
- Uses Indian English naturally: *"gyaan," "lala jobs," "crore"* — no self-consciousness about it.
- Mixes rupee symbols (₹) with English text without explanation. Assumes the reader is Indian or context-aware.
- No hedging language. No *"it could be argued"* or *"one might consider."* States everything as fact, then backs it with observation.
- Uses "I" constantly — first-person is the default, not the exception.

---

## How Posts Close

- Ends with either a one-line lesson that sounds like a proverb (*"Marketing sells the myth. Formulation decides the truth."*) or a direct question aimed at the reader (*"Are you building a business or waiting to be replaced by one?"*).
- Closing lines are short — under 12 words. No long summaries. No "In conclusion."
- Sometimes closes with a binary choice or contrast structure: *"If you want money → go to a big market. If you want impact → stay in a growing market."*
- Occasionally adds a P.S. to plug a client or personal note — kept to one line, never the emotional center.

---

## Structure Patterns

- Heavy use of numbered lists (1–12 items) for tactical or observational posts. Lists are not padded — each point is 1–2 lines max.
- Uses bullet points with dashes or arrows (→) when showing cause-and-effect or before/after contrast.
- Frequently structures posts as: **Bold claim → Personal story or observation → Reframe → Lesson.**
- Uses white space aggressively. Single-line paragraphs are common. Never writes a wall of text.
- Uses the word "But" or "And" to start sentences often — treats them as rhythm tools, not grammar violations.

---

## Vocabulary He Uses

- *"Uncomfortable," "realised," "tailwind," "leverage" (as a noun, not a verb), "moat," "infra," "cognition," "optimise," "mid-transition"*
- Favors plain verbs: *use, build, kill, break, eat, hit, run, stay, act.*
- Uses rhetorical contrast pairs: *smart vs. not-so-smart, money vs. impact, myth vs. truth, label vs. formulation.*
- Uses → arrows instead of writing "leads to" or "results in."
- Writes numbers as digits always: *₹500, 10+, 12 credit cards, 5–10x.*

---

## Words and Phrases He Never Uses

- Never writes "utilize" — always "use."
- Never writes "innovative," "synergy," "ecosystem" in a positive/buzzword way.
- Never writes "I hope this helps" or "Let me know your thoughts" — he ends with a sharp statement or direct question, not a courtesy close.
- Never uses hashtags within body copy. (If used at all, would be at the very end — not mid-post.)
- Never uses em-dashes (—) in the middle of sentences to add asides. Uses full stops instead.
- Avoids passive voice almost entirely.

---

## Patterns That Make This Voice Distinct

1. **The personal proof point before the argument.** He doesn't argue from theory — he argues from something that happened to him. *"I have a cheap perfume called Spruce Shave Club"* before making a point about the entire industry.
2. **Contrarian framing that respects both sides.** He doesn't mock people who believe the wrong thing. He shows how the system created the belief. *"This is not about BJP vs Congress. This is about system design vs ground reality."*
3. **Category-level thinking in personal posts.** Even when sharing something personal (why he started his company), he pulls it up to a market or societal observation before the end.
4. **Stalin-level references next to startup advice.** Mixes high-culture references (war literature, Stalin quotes) with ₹500 perfume comparisons without irony. It works because the contrast is unannounced.
5. **Ends listicles with the one point that reframes all the others.** In the startup mistakes post, the last point is *"Don't die"* — a tonal shift that makes the whole list feel human.

## Hook Pattern by Post Type

| Post Type | Hook Behavior |
|---|---|
| **Unpopular Opinion / Contrarian** | One-line claim that most people would instinctively disagree with. Stated without softening. |
| **Founder Pain / Relatable Rant** | Opens with a personal admission that feels slightly embarrassing. *"I had 12 credit cards."* |
| **Broken System / Exposé** | Names the industry and the lie in the same sentence. No buildup. |
| **Global vs. India** | Starts with the global behavior, then pivots with "But here" or "Here it's different." |
| **Emotionally Raw / Reflection** | Opens with a numbered list of "I" statements. No story setup — jumps into the list immediately. |
| **Hidden Cost / Awareness** | Starts with the surface-level common belief, then one word: *"Reality:"* |
| **Lesson from the Trenches** | Starts with the conclusion, not the story. Story comes second, as proof. |

---

## Audience Assumptions Baked Into the Writing

- Assumes the reader is Indian, urban, educated, mid-career or founder-stage.
- Does not explain Indian references (CRED, Gurugram, lala jobs, GCC). Writes for people who already know.
- Treats the reader as intelligent but possibly wrong. Never condescending — more like *"I thought the same thing until I looked closer."*
- Pitches himself as someone who has been inside the system and left it — not an outsider critic.

---

## What to Replicate When Writing in This Voice

- Start with the conclusion. Earn the story after.
- Keep every sentence under 12 words unless you're making a list.
- Use "But" and "And" to start sentences when you need a beat shift.
- Never explain the punchline. State it and stop.
- One lesson per post. No listicle of lessons — one clear thing the reader should walk away believing.
- Read the closing line aloud. If it takes more than 4 seconds to say, cut it in half.
