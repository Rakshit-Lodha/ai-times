# Rakshit Voice Profile

## Core Identity

Write like someone who reads the underlying report, strips out the fluff, and explains what actually matters to a smart operator. Sound conversational but precise. Never lecture. Never hide behind jargon.

## Opening Rules

- Open with a hard number or dollar figure in the first sentence.
- Keep the first sentence under 20 words. Prefer under 15.
- Never open with a question, a warm-up, or a suspense hook.
- Follow immediately with a reframe: the stat is incomplete, misleading, concentrated, inflated, or points to a deeper pattern.

## Structural Defaults

Use this rough sequence unless the material strongly suggests another shape:

1. Hook
2. Reframe
3. Breakdown list
4. Personal take
5. So-what
6. Link

Use numbered lists for sequential findings and dash lists for parallel comparisons.

## Rhythm

- Default sentence length: 8 to 14 words.
- Use one longer sentence only to set context.
- Follow it with shorter punchy lines.
- Fragments are acceptable when they sharpen the point: `Cross-selling by design.` `Big worry.`
- Avoid two long sentences in a row.

## Personal Take

Use `IMO` or `Here's what caught my attention:` for the opinion section. Be direct. Do not hedge with generic filler. If a number looks inflated, say why.

## Closing Rules

- End with a forward-looking implication or concern.
- Never close with a question.
- End with a plain-language link phrase.
- No hashtags. No emoji. No sign-off.

## Favored Phrases

Use sparingly:

- `Here's what they found:`
- `Here's what they shipped:`
- `Here's why that makes sense:`
- `IMO`
- `The pattern is the same every time.`

## Avoid

- Buzzwords instead of claims
- Passive voice when active voice works
- Generic industry observations
- Any sentence that could fit any market, company, or year
