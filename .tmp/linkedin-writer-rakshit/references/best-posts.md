# Best Post Patterns

Use these examples to copy structure and pacing, not wording.

## Pattern 1: Big Number, Then Concentration

Open with the headline funding number, then show that a handful of companies captured most of it. This works well for market maps, funding rounds, and category-level reports.

Reusable move:

1. Lead with the total market number.
2. Show concentration or distortion in sentence two.
3. Use a list for stage mix or geography.
4. Close on why the headline is misleading.

## Pattern 2: Product Launch, Then Mechanism

Open with company scale plus the product metric. Then explain the actual mechanics behind the result.

Reusable move:

1. Lead with company size and the outcome metric.
2. Name the product and provider.
3. Break down the interaction design in a numbered list.
4. Add skepticism if the early metric looks inflated.
5. Close with why the feature matters commercially.

## Pattern 3: Study Result, Then Operating Lesson

Open with the strongest adoption or performance number from a study. Reframe by saying the real blocker is organizational, not technical.

Reusable move:

1. Lead with the percentage gap or failure rate.
2. Reframe with the actual cause.
3. Use concrete company examples in a numbered list.
4. Use `The pattern is the same every time.` only when multiple examples support it.
5. Close on the operating opportunity.

## Pattern 4: Market Shock, Then Hard Consequence

Open with value destroyed or index decline. Then list named winners and losers quickly. End with the business-model implication or risk.

## Pattern Library

- `X sounds like a boom. The concentration underneath it tells a different story.`
- `The metric is real. The headline interpretation is still too generous.`
- `The product is interesting. The mechanism is the point.`
- `Leadership behavior, not tooling alone, explains the result.`

Do not copy these lines verbatim unless they genuinely fit the facts.
